    Honors And Awards 
        
  • Dec. 2016 : Second Prize of National Cryptography Math Challenge( held in 2016).
    • 
        The 1-st contest contain 3 main problems and I chose the third problem -- Attacks On Weak RSA System. The question
    gave us 21 cipher texts who were encripted by 21 different pairs of (e,N). The massages are padded to 512 bits and Ns
    are up to 1024 bits.
        The first time I saw the question I thought it is impossible to factor all the big integer N. However, it is a
    CONTEST such that there must be some weak prime factors or some other tricks. So with some tips mentioned in reference,
    I did some trivial check like whether there are some pair of N share one same prime? In this approach, I found there
    were actually two N share same prime and two N are same! So Common Modulus Attack came into force.
        Then I tried all the attacks to RSA(CRT attack, Copper-Smith Attack, p-1 algorithm, Rho algorithm), some works,
    some didn't work. But as the first time to join a contest, I felt it amazing to gain second prize.

  • Jan. 2018 : First Prize of National Cryptography Math Challenge( held in 2017).
    • 
        The 2-ed contest contain 4 main problems and I chose the problem two -- 「Math Problems in Correlation Attacks」.
    The question is to calculate the initial value(or called seed) of a LFSR. The question just gave us a binary
    sequence gained by transferring through a BSC(Binary symmetric channel) with crossover probability p. When you get some
    initial values, the lower p is, the higher scores you get.
        Facing this problem, I have no experience at all. But after one year to practice, I find python is elegant
    programming language since it can be vary short and easy to debug. Preparing the programming language and its IDE(Spyder).
    I began to read all the papers the question mentioned.
        Although I found it finally need a faster language such like C++, python did a lot of work on the sketch of the
    algorithm. After reading all 4 papers, I found the last algorithm mentioned in the last paper-"Improved fast correlation
    attack using low rate codes." is most powerful. It is improved correlation at- tack based on maximum likelihood
    decoding. Using this algorithm, the 9/12 of the data are encrypted.
